Knowledge Base

Foreign Buyer's Guide

Everything you need to know about buying real estate in Mexico as a foreigner.

01 —

Fideicomiso Structure

Foreigners cannot directly own land in Mexico's "Restricted Zone" (within 50km of coast). A fideicomiso is a legal trust where a Mexican bank holds title on your behalf. You retain full ownership rights, can sell freely, and pass it to heirs. No restrictions.

02 —

Costs & Financing

Total closing costs are typically 5-8% (notary, registration, fideicomiso setup). Many foreign buyers finance locally with Mexican banks or SOFIMEX (foreign-friendly lenders) at competitive rates. We connect you with trusted financing partners.

03 —

Timeline & Process

From discovery to closing: 4-8 weeks. Our legal team handles all documentation. You'll need: valid passport, proof of funds, and a few signatures. The rest is on us.

04 —

Resale & Appreciation

Your property is fully yours to sell, rent, or pass on. Mérida properties appreciate ~15% annually on average. Rental income is strong, especially near expat zones and beach communities.

05 —

Residency & Community

Buying property doesn't automatically grant residency, but you can apply for Temporary or Permanent Residency separately. Mérida has a thriving expat community with English services, international schools, and clubs.

06 —

Safety & Stability

Mérida ranks #1 safest large city in Mexico (6 years). Your investment is protected by Mexican law. We stay current on regulations and ensure all transactions are legally sound.
