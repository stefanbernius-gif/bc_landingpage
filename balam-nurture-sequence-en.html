Fideicomiso? It's Not What You Think (Explained in 5 min)

Hi [NAME],

If you're a foreigner thinking about buying in Mexico, you'll hear one word that sounds terrifying: fideicomiso.

"Is it risky?" "Could I lose my money?" "What if something goes wrong?"

Here's the truth: A fideicomiso is simply a legal instrument that PROTECTS your investment. It's required for foreigners in the Coastal Zone (within 50 km of the ocean), and it's your best ally.

How it works (without the legal jargon):

  • You provide the money (you're the fideicomiso beneficiary)
  • A bank acts as fiduciary (neutral intermediary)
  • The property is registered in the fideicomiso's name (for your benefit)
  • YOU have ALL rights: sell, inherit, live in, rent out
  • When you sell, the fideicomiso dissolves and you get your money back

What matters: This is NOT a loss of control. It's protection. Many countries require something similar for foreigners. In Yucatán, it's standard and secure.

At Balam Group, our legal team handles the entire fideicomiso formation. You just sign in the right places. Simple.
