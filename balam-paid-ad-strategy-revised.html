Research Findings

Why Awareness-First Makes Sense for $500–600 Budget

SEO Gap Analysis (200-Keyword Study)

Critical Finding: Balam dominates branded/decision keywords but fails on awareness-stage content.

High-volume awareness keywords where Balam underperforms:

  • "escuelas cerca de mi" → 110K monthly searches, Rank 24 (target: Top 10)
  • "escuelas primarias cerca de mi" → 40.5K searches, Rank 26 (target: Top 10)
  • "colegios cerca de mi" → 40.5K searches, Rank 27 (target: Top 10)
  • "pet friendly Mérida Yucatán" → 18.1K searches, Rank 96 (massive gap)
  • "Chuburna Puerto Yucatán" → 14.8K searches, Rank 20–25 (good but improvable)
  • "haciendas en Yucatán" → 720 searches, Rank 72 (poor)

Content type performance:

  • Proyecto/desarrollo pages: Avg Rank 19.8 (strong — no ads needed)
  • Blog/guía pages: Avg Rank 36.4 (weak — ads can bridge this gap)
  • Decision-stage keywords: Avg Rank 22 (strong organic)
  • Awareness-stage keywords: Avg Rank 41 (weak — ads are cost-effective here)

Budget Efficiency: Why $600 Beats $3K

High-intent keywords are expensive: Fideicomiso, "buy property," property lawyers cost $2–5 per click.

Awareness keywords are affordable: Schools, neighborhoods, lifestyle, pet-friendly cost $0.50–1.50 per click (3–5x cheaper).

$3K budget on high-intent keywords:
CPC average: $3 | Monthly clicks: 1,000 | Expected leads (3% conversion): 30 | Cost per lead: $100

$600 budget on awareness keywords:
CPC average: $1 | Monthly clicks: 600 | Expected leads (8–10% conversion, warm traffic): 48–60 | Cost per lead: $10–12.50

Plus: Awareness traffic to blog pages improves SEO ranking → Organic traffic increases → Ad dependency decreases over 6 months.

Phase 5: SEO Synergy

How Awareness Ads Improve Organic Ranking

The feedback loop:

1. Run awareness ads → Blog traffic increases

2. Blog readers engage (1.5+ min on page, internal link clicks)
   → Google sees signals: user satisfaction, engagement time

3. Engagement signals improve page authority
   → Rankings improve for "escuelas," "pet friendly," "neighborhoods"

4. As blog pages rank better organically → Reduce ad spend

5. Organic traffic takes over → Lower CAC long-term

Example trajectory:

  • Month 1: 500 blog visits via ads + 200 organic = 700 total
  • Month 3: 600 blog visits via ads + 800 organic = 1,400 total (organic doubles)
  • Month 6: 400 blog visits via ads + 2,000 organic = 2,400 total (organic takes over)
Result: By Month 6, same blog traffic at lower ad cost because organic has improved.

Why $600 Makes Sense

1. Lower-Intent Keywords Are Cheaper

"Escuelas cerca de mi": CPC $0.50–1.00 (vs. "buy property" at $3–5) = 6x more clicks for same budget

2. Blog Traffic Builds Organic Ranking

Each month of ad-driven traffic improves SEO. After 6 months, organic traffic reduces ad reliance. $600 × 6 months = $3,600 total investment with lasting SEO benefit.

3. Risk Reduction

Test awareness funnel at low cost first. Once blog pages rank better, add high-intent budget. Proof of concept before scaling to $2K+.

4. Budget Reality

$600/month is more defensible than $3K/month. Shows commitment to testing, not large upfront risk. Can scale if results prove ROI.
