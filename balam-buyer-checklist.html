📋 Before You Start (Financial Prep)

Get your finances in order before reaching out. This ensures smooth closing and faster timelines.
  • Determine your budget Including all costs: property price + 8-10% for fideicomiso, legal, taxes, and title insurance
  • Verify your financing Have funds ready via international wire transfer. Most closes happen in 8-10 weeks.
  • Get a valid passport Required for all legal documents. Must be valid for at least 1 year beyond closing date.
  • Understand fideicomiso It's a legal trust that protects foreign buyers. Not scary — it's industry standard.
  • Clarify your investment intent Are you buying to live in, invest/rent, or flip? This affects property selection.

💬 During Your Consultation Call

We'll ask these questions to understand your needs and recommend the right properties.
  • Have a timeline in mind When do you want to close? 6 months? 1 year? This affects property selection and urgency.
  • Know your neighborhood preferences Downtown (walkable), North (expat-friendly), South (family-oriented), or Beachfront (resort area)?
  • List your must-haves vs nice-to-haves Pool? Guest house? Parking? Proximity to restaurants/beach? Prioritize what matters most.
  • Ask about property appreciation Mérida north/south has appreciated 3-4% annually. We'll show you data by neighborhood.
  • Clarify the full cost breakdown Ask us to detail: property price + commission + fideicomiso + legal + taxes + title insurance

🏠 Before Your Property Viewing Trip

Prepare for your 2-3 day trip to Mérida. Bring documents and have a clear idea of what you're looking for.
  • Book your flights & accommodations We recommend 2-3 days minimum. We can suggest hotels in different neighborhoods so you can feel the vibe.
  • Review property listings beforehand We'll send 5-7 properties that match your budget. Review photos/details before arriving.
  • Bring valid identification Passport is preferred. Some properties may require ID to enter (security measure).
  • Plan your schedule with flexibility We typically show 4-6 properties per day. Leave room for spontaneous neighborhood walks.
  • Bring a list of questions Ask about: HOA fees (if applicable), utilities, property taxes, insurance, rental potential, resale history

🔍 What to Check During Property Viewings

  • Inspect the property condition Roof, plumbing, electrical, AC units, windows, doors. Ask about age and recent repairs.
  • Check the neighborhood vibe Walk around. Talk to neighbors. Visit at different times of day. Feel the energy.
  • Verify proximity to amenities Restaurants, banks, grocery stores, hospitals, beaches. Google Maps before/after viewing.
  • Ask about title and legal status Is the property clear? Any liens or disputes? We verify everything, but ask directly.
  • Take photos/videos for your records Review later with family/advisors. It helps with decision-making.
  • Ask about rental potential (if investing) Average nightly rate? Occupancy rates? What's the ROI timeline?
💡 Pro Tip: Don't decide same day. Sleep on it. Call us the next day with your top 1-2 choices. Rushing leads to regrets.

✍️ Legal Process & Closing (Weeks 1-10)

Once you choose a property, we handle the paperwork. Here's what happens behind the scenes.
  • Title verification (Weeks 1-2) Our legal team reviews the property title for any liens, disputes, or issues. You'll get a clear report.
  • Fideicomiso setup (Weeks 2-4) We set up the trust agreement with the bank. This protects you as a foreign buyer.
  • Contract finalization (Weeks 3-4) Seller agreement is drafted, reviewed, and signed by both parties. No surprises at this stage.
  • Wire your funds (Weeks 5-6) Send payment via international wire. We provide exact instructions and banking details.
  • Notary signing (Weeks 7-8) You can sign remotely or in person. We provide all documents and clear instructions.
  • Title registration (Weeks 9-10) Property is registered in your name. You get keys and full ownership.

🚩 Red Flags to Watch Out For

  • Pressure to decide quickly Real estate shouldn't be rushed. If someone pressures you, walk away.
  • Unclear or incomplete cost breakdown You should know EVERY cost upfront. No surprises at closing.
  • Title problems or missing documents Liens, unclear ownership, squatters, or paperwork gaps? We don't move forward until resolved.
  • Agent won't let you inspect property alone You should feel free to walk around, take photos, and ask locals questions.
  • Unusually low prices for the area If a property seems too good to be true, it probably is. Ask why it's priced that way.

✅ After Closing (What Comes Next)

  • Receive your ownership documents Official registration from the Mexican government. This is your proof of ownership.
  • Get your keys and take possession Property is now legally yours. Set up utilities if needed (electricity, water, internet).
  • Set up property insurance Highly recommended. We can connect you with local insurers.
  • Plan your next steps (if renting) If investing, set up rental management. We recommend local property managers.
  • Stay connected with us We support you beyond closing. Questions about maintenance, tax planning, or selling? We're here.
