# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

English-speaking foreign buyers and expats — primarily from the US, Canada, and Europe — considering relocating to or investing in Mérida, Yucatán. They are not familiar with the Mexican real estate process, may be nervous about buying property abroad, and need a trusted guide who speaks their language (literally and figuratively). They arrive from search or referral, often having read about Mérida's safety and growth story, and are evaluating multiple agencies. Decision-making timeline is weeks to months.

## Product Purpose

A high-converting English-language landing page for Balam Group Real Estate that turns curious foreign visitors into qualified leads. Success = a visitor submits a contact form, downloads the free guide, or sends a WhatsApp message. The page must establish Balam as the authoritative, human, trustworthy partner for navigating Mérida real estate — not just another listings database.

## Brand Personality

Trusted. Premium. Local.

Warm expertise: Balam knows Mérida deeply and will guide you through every step. Not cold fine-art-gallery luxury, not a generic portal. The voice is confident, calm, and personal — like getting advice from a well-connected friend who happens to know the city inside out.

## Anti-references

- **Generic MLS / Zillow / realtor.com grid**: avoid the sea-of-listings, filter-heavy, database-style design. This page is not a search tool.
- **Sotheby's / cold-luxury**: avoid the overly sparse, white-space-for-its-own-sake minimalism that feels untouchable and alienating to a buyer who needs hand-holding.

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence through specificity** — Real numbers, real neighborhoods, real client names. Vague superlatives ("the best in Mérida") mean nothing; specific proof ("500+ foreign buyers since 2012") converts.
2. **Guide, don't sell** — The page should feel like expert advice, not a pitch. Educational sections (why Mérida now, how to buy as a foreigner) build trust before asking for anything.
3. **Human before architectural** — Lead with people (testimonials, team, client stories), then show properties. Expats buy from people they trust, not from buildings.
4. **One decision at a time** — Each section resolves one doubt (Is Mérida right for me? Can foreigners buy? Who are these people?) before presenting the next CTA.
5. **Bilingual-ready, English-first** — All visible UI strings in English. Never mix languages within a section. Spanish can appear in property names and neighborhood names as proper nouns only.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (color contrast, keyboard navigation, alt text on all images).
- All CTAs clearly labeled (no icon-only buttons).
- Form fields with visible labels (not just placeholder text).
