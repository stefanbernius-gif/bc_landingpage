Paid Digital Audit Pricing Guarantee: What to Expect from Pricing, Process, and Guarantees

July 18, 2026 · 8 min read · Audits & ROI

Paid digital audits typically cost between $500 and $7,500, depending on scope and provider. But price alone tells you little — across a sample of prominent digital audit providers, only a handful actually attach a paid digital audit pricing guarantee to their findings. Most treat audits as sales bait, not diagnostics. This piece breaks down realistic paid digital audit pricing and guarantee structures so you know what you're actually buying.

Key Takeaways

  • Quality audits start at $500 — anything less is typically automated and lacks real strategy.
  • Most agencies treat audits as lead generation, not standalone diagnostic products with guarantees.
  • Only a handful of providers offer a money-back or value guarantee on audit findings.
  • A credible audit includes a scored report, prioritized roadmap, and financial justification — not just an issue list.

The Real Price of a Paid Digital Audit (and Why Free Audits Cost You More)

Market pricing clusters into three tiers. Freelancer or lite audits run $500–$2,500. Full strategic audits run $2,500–$7,500. Enterprise audits — think full technical, content, and link analysis at scale — run $10,000–$20,000+.

Anything under $500 should raise questions. At that price, you're almost always getting an automated scan: a tool-generated PDF with no human judgment, no prioritization, and no roadmap. It's not an audit — it's a report generator with your logo removed.

Here's the part most businesses miss: free audits aren't cheaper, they're just deferred cost. Agencies offer them as sales pitches, structured to justify a retainer, not to give you an honest diagnosis. The incentive is to find "problems" that happen to match whatever service they sell — not necessarily the problems costing you the most revenue.

A paid audit flips the incentive. You're paying for the diagnosis itself, independent of what happens next. That's the entire premise behind treating an audit as a product, not a pitch.

Bernius Consulting pricing reflects this split explicitly:

  • Proof Sprint — $497: one pillar (Conversion Architecture), four sub-checks scored, three quick wins, and a 10–12 minute Loom walkthrough — delivered in 24 hours.
  • Full 360 Audit — $2,497: all seven pillars, 28+ scored sub-checks, competitor paid-media scan, six-month financial model, and 60-minute strategy call — delivered in 48 hours.

Both sit inside the "full strategic audit" range the broader market charges — but with fixed pricing and no discovery-call sales theater.

What a Legitimate Audit Delivers: 7 Non-Negotiable Components

If an audit doesn't include these seven elements, you're likely paying for a checklist, not a diagnosis.

  1. Scored report per pillar (0–100) — tied to revenue impact, not just traffic or technical metrics.
  2. Revenue-gap estimates per sub-check and per pillar — showing the dollar amount left on the table, not just "issues found."
  3. Competitor paid-media scan — revealing where rivals are outspending or out-targeting you.
  4. Ranked action items with financial justification — every recommendation tied to a dollar estimate, not a generic best practice.
  5. Implementation roadmap with clear sequencing — what to fix first, second, third, and why that order matters financially.
  6. Three-scenario six-month financial model — conservative, moderate, and aggressive projections of recovered revenue.
  7. Money-back guarantee — a full refund if the audit fails to identify at least $8,000/month in recoverable revenue.

Most competing audits stop at component one or two — a list of technical issues, maybe a score. Few tie findings to dollar figures, and almost none attach a guarantee that's measurable rather than vague.

The distinction matters because a scored, dollar-denominated report gives you something a checklist never can: a basis for prioritization. If everything is "important," nothing gets fixed. If Item A is worth $3,200/month and Item B is worth $400/month, the decision makes itself.

Guarantees That Actually Mean Something: Separating Promises from Proof

Guarantee language in this space falls into three buckets: vague value guarantees, subjective satisfaction guarantees, or no guarantee at all. Most providers researched fall into the third category.

Among those that do offer something, examples include providers claiming a "5x value guarantee" where the audit is free if it doesn't deliver a multiple of its cost in perceived value, or a "100% value guarantee" tied to a fixed-price audit. These are common marketing promises, but "value" is subjective — who decides if the threshold was met? A dollar-denominated revenue threshold isn't. Either the audit identifies $8,000/month in recoverable revenue across the scored pillars, or it doesn't.

Bernius Consulting guarantees identification of at least $8,000/month in recoverable revenue in the Full 360 Audit — determined solely from audit findings, not contingent on implementation — and issues a full refund if that threshold isn't met. This guarantee applies specifically to the Full 360 Audit, not the Proof Sprint.

The Proof Sprint carries a separate, limited guarantee: it is fully credited toward the Full 360 Audit if purchased within 30 days. There is no cash refund on the Sprint itself; the guarantee is credit toward the full-scope engagement.

Before hiring any provider, ask directly: "What happens if the audit doesn't find enough revenue to justify the cost?" Most agencies stumble here, because most never built an answer into their pricing model.

Choose the Proof Sprint if: you already suspect your conversion path is broken — a weak funnel, poor form completion, unclear calls-to-action — and want low-cost confirmation before committing further.

Guessing wrong on a single-pillar audit wastes more time than starting comprehensive.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a paid digital audit cost?

Quality audits range from $500 to $7,500 depending on depth and business complexity. Bernius offers a $497 Proof Sprint and a $2,497 Full 360 Audit, both fixed-price with no discovery-call negotiation.

What's the difference between a free audit and a paid audit?

Free audits are lead-generation tools — automated, surface-level, designed to sell a retainer. Paid audits are standalone diagnostic products with scored findings, financial justification, and actionable roadmaps independent of future service purchases.

Are there audits with a money-back or value guarantee?

Few providers offer explicit guarantees. Bernius guarantees identification of at least $8,000/month in recoverable revenue for the Full 360 Audit, or a full refund — a measurable threshold rather than a subjective "value" claim.

What should a paid audit deliver?

At minimum: a scored report, ranked action items with financial justification, competitor intelligence, and a prioritized implementation roadmap tied to revenue impact — not just a list of technical issues.

How do I know if an audit provider is credible?

Ask for sample deliverables, a clear breakdown of what's scored, and what happens if the audit underdelivers. Credible providers answer the refund question directly; most others deflect.

Final Thoughts

A paid digital audit is a diagnostic investment, not a cost — but only when findings tie directly to revenue recovery. A measurable, money-back guarantee separates a genuine diagnostic product from a dressed-up sales pitch. If a provider can't tell you what happens when the audit falls short, that's your answer.
